I am a PhD student in the Exploratory Data Analysis Group at the Max Planck Institute for Informatics. I completed my M.Sc. degree in Computer Science from Saarland University, Germany. Before that I did my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Engineering from Pulchowk campus, Tribhuvan University, Nepal. I am primarily interested in Exploratory Data Mining. In particular, I develop methods for mining interesting patterns from data with the aid of Information Theory.

I have greatly benefited from free courses & materials available online. Among many, Introduction to Algorithms (OCW MIT) was the one I enjoyed the most. I have been so touched by the last lecture of Randy Pausch: Achieving Your Childhood Dreams & Walter Lewin: For the love of Physics. You might find this 3 minute talk delivered by Derek Sivers in TED conference interesting: How to start a movement. If you have some free time to think about the progress of applied side of computer science, you might as well find this discussion interesting.

Every now and then, I write about things that I find interesting here.
